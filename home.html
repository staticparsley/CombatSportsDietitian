    Weightcutting and Fractures in MMA

    High protein diet in rats reduced bone mineral density due to metabolic acidosis, the opposite was found in humans. This is a good example on how animal studies don’t transfer to humans.
    Unfortunately calcium absorption capacity has been shown to decline with weight loss. The smaller the person, the thinner the bones. Muscle is the primary source of anabolic mechanical stimuli for bone tissue. Adiponectin, which is inversely associated with fat mass, effects bone mineral density. When weight loss stabilizes after one year, bone mineral density is still found to decline up until year 2. In general, the weight loss groups have experienced decreases in body weight of 9% to 10% and decreases in BMD of about 2%. Finally, a disquieting observation from prior studies is that when bone mass is lost during weight loss, it does not seem to be regained in full when weight is regained. This mirrors the observation that the BMD decline during spaceflight and the microarchitectural deterioration sustained during non–weight-bearing after orthopedic surgery do not recover in full after return to normal weight-bearing activity resumes.
    This indicates caloric restriction increase fracture risk and are regulated by endocrine factors and cytokines that have direct and indirect effects on bone and calcium absorption. The hormones released from meal ingestion appear to have an anabolic effect on bone. Additionally, a higher protein intake increases Ca absorption, which thus may attenuate the decrease in Ca absorption associated with caloric restriction. Energy restriction also reduces calcium absorption efficiency
    Reduced energy to fracture has also been seen in dehydrated bones, think of toast vs bread.
    Jockeys tend to have osteopenia.
    Elite 10/11 grade high school wrestlers had reduced bone mineral density early in the season during weight cutting than post season.
    College Wrestlers and judoka have higher bone mineral density than endurance athletes and non athletes, this shows the benefits of weight bearing exercises even with weight cycling.
    Judokas were obsessed having markers of bone mineral density during weight loss.
    Acute fractures comprise 5% to 10% of all sports injuries. Overall, MMA is a sport with a high injury rate, with an incidence of 25 injuries per 100 competitive fights. Out of which about 6% are fractures, so this is 1.5% of bouts
